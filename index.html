Problem 1 – Sound and the Sprint Start

Two sprinters are separated by 3.66 m. The pistol fires near Lane 1. Assume standard speed of sound. In official results, A finishes in 9.784 s, B in 9.789 s.

(a) If they reacted to the real gunshot (no speakers), by how much earlier would the closer runner hear the sound?
(b) Using that time advantage, which runner would win and by how much?

Show Hint
Time diff = \( \Delta t = \frac{\Delta d}{v_{sound}} \). Compare this \( \Delta t \) to the original winning margin.

Problem 2 – Stressed Rail

A steel rail is held rigidly. \( E = 195 \, \text{GPa} \), \( \alpha = 1.63 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{K}^{-1} \), \( \sigma_{\max} = 600 \, \text{MPa} \).

Question: What maximum temperature change \( \Delta T_{\max} \) can the rail withstand before hitting max stress?

Show Hint
Combine \( \sigma = E \epsilon \) and \( \epsilon = \alpha \Delta T \). Solve for \( \Delta T \).

Problem 3 – Shadow Walking

Lamp height \( H = 3.2 \, \text{m} \). Person height \( h = 1.8 \, \text{m} \). Walking speed \( v = 1.5 \, \text{m/s} \).

(a) Express shadow length \( x \) in terms of distance \( s \).
(b) Write \( x(t) \) explicitly.
(c) What is the acceleration of the tip of the shadow?

Show Hint
Similar triangles: \( \frac{s+x}{H} = \frac{x}{h} \). If \( s(t) \) is linear (constant velocity), what kind of function is \( x(t) \)?

Problem 4 – Thumb-Rule Plane Spotting

Rule: "1 thumb = \( 2.5^\circ \)". Plane is in view for 10s, sweeps 7.5 thumbs. Plane appears 0.5 thumbs long. Actual length = 40m.

Question: Estimate the plane's speed in km/h.

Show Hint
Angular size \( \theta \approx \frac{\text{length}}{\text{distance}} \). Total distance traveled \( \approx \frac{\text{total angle}}{\text{angular size}} \times \text{plane length} \).
